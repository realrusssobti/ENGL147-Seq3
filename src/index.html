Our Values

Automation

We strongly believe that automation with AI is a force for good. By increasing the safety of AI systems, humanity will be more productive as a whole and advance in a multitude of issues.

Equity

We believe that AI systems should be evaluated from the same criteria for diversity, equity, and inclusion as humans. When dealing with sensitive groups of people, care should be taken to make sure that AI is not unintentionally marginalizing people.

Objectivity

We believe that automated decision-making systems have the potential to improve the quality of decision-making across industries. Hooli believes that AI removes human biases when care is taken to avoid reproducing the same bias inside the dataset or AI model. 

Case Study: Tay.AI

In 2016, Microsoft unveiled a chatbot called Tay.ai, which was a trained Twitter chatbot that used cutting-edge deep learning technologies. While Tay was not directly used for business objectives, the users of Twitter were able to "troll" the AI. Due to a lack of data filtering, this led Tay to express racist, sexist, xenophobic, and highly controversial opinions. Tay was promptly shut down.
